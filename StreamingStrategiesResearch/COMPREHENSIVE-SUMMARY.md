### The Three C's of Streaming Success

**1. Consistency (40% of success)**
- Regular schedule (same days, same times)

- Algorithm rewards consistency
- Audience habit formation

**2. Content Quality (35%)**
- Clear audio (MOST important)
- Valuable information or entertainment
- Proper pacing
- Professional presentation

**3. Community Building (25%)**
- Active engagement
- Off-stream presence
- Recognition of regulars
- Shared culture

### Technical Priority Investments

**Priority 1: Audio Quality (MOST CRITICAL)**
- Minimum: USB microphone ($50-100)
- Recommended: Blue Yeti, Audio-Technica AT2020 ($100-200)
- Professional: Shure SM7B with interface ($200+)
- Settings: Noise suppression, gate at -40dB, 160kbps bitrate

**Priority 2: Internet Connection**
- Minimum: 10 Mbps upload for 1080p/60fps
- Wired ethernet (never WiFi for streaming)
- Test with speedtest.net
- Leave 30-40% overhead

**Priority 3: Video Quality**
- Minimum: 720p @ 30fps
- Recommended: 1080p @ 60fps
- Camera: Logitech C920/C922 ($50-100) or better
- Lighting: Ring light or softbox ($30-100)

**Priority 4: Software Setup**
- OBS Studio (free, most flexible)
- Scenes: Starting Soon, Main, BRB, Ending
- Bitrate: 6000 kbps (Twitch), 8000-12000 kbps (YouTube)
- Encoder: x264 medium or NVENC (GPU)

### Perfect Stream Structure

**Pre-Stream (15 min before):**
- Go live with "Starting Soon" screen
- Play music
- Monitor chat for tech issues
- Prepare mentally

**Opening (5-10 min):**
- Enthusiastic greeting
- Today's agenda
- Audio/video check
- Engage early viewers
- Set expectations

**Main Content (Bulk):**
- 20-30 min focused work segments
- 5 min discussion/Q&A segments
- Regular recaps every 15-20 minutes
- Celebrate small wins
- Ask for input

**Breaks (Every 60-90 min):**
- Announce 5 minutes before
- 5-10 minutes max
- Keep music playing
- Return with energy

**Ending (10-15 min):**
- Recap accomplishments
- Show before/after
- Thank contributors
- Preview next stream
- Clear CTA (follow/subscribe)
- Raid/host another channel
